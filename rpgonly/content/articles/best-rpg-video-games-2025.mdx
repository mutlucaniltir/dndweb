The RPG genre has never been richer. Between Larian's CRPG renaissance, FromSoftware redefining action RPGs, and Square Enix still pushing Final Fantasy, there's more quality than you can possibly play in one lifetime.

This list focuses on what you should actually prioritize in 2025 — games that represent the peak of what RPGs can do. We've ranked them by overall quality and lasting relevance, not just recency.

## The Rankings

### 1. Baldur's Gate 3

There's no debate. Baldur's Gate 3 is the greatest CRPG ever made, and one of the best games of all time across any genre. If you haven't played it, stop reading and start downloading.

What Larian achieved is staggering: a 100+ hour RPG where your choices genuinely matter, built on actual D&D 5e rules, with companions that feel like real people, full co-op, and multiple endings that change based on decisions made hours earlier.

The writing alone would make it great. The systems, encounter design, and sheer reactivity make it generational.

### 2. Elden Ring

FromSoftware's open-world evolution of the Souls formula was so ambitious it shouldn't have worked. It did, and it set a new benchmark for open-world game design.

Elden Ring is a brutal, rewarding action RPG where the world itself tells the story. The Lands Between is dense with hidden content, lore fragments, and encounters that reward exploration. Every legacy dungeon (Stormveil, Leyndell, Farum Azula) is a masterpiece of level design.

The RPG systems — stat allocation, weapon scaling, ashes of war — are deep enough to support dozens of viable builds.

<Callout type="warning" title="Difficulty Warning">
Elden Ring is not casual-friendly. It will kill you. Repeatedly. The design ethos is that death is a teacher, not a failure state. If that sounds good to you, it's the best game of the decade. If not, start with Baldur's Gate 3 or Final Fantasy instead.
</Callout>

### 3. Final Fantasy XIV (with Endwalker)

If you want thousands of hours of content, a passionate community, and one of the best stories in gaming, FFXIV is your answer.

The MMO went through a disastrous launch and rebirth (A Realm Reborn) to become one of gaming's greatest redemption arcs. The Shadowbringers and Endwalker expansions in particular deliver story content that rivals standalone single-player RPGs.

The free trial is extraordinarily generous — you can play through A Realm Reborn and the Heavensward expansion for free, which is 150+ hours of content.

### 4. Disco Elysium: The Final Cut

The most unique RPG ever made. You play an amnesiac detective solving a murder case in a decaying, politically complex city. There is no combat. Everything resolves through skill checks — and the skills argue with each other inside your head.

Disco Elysium is a masterclass in using game systems to tell story. It has more to say about politics, memory, failure, and the human condition than most literature.

**Note:** Disco Elysium's development studio was shut down in 2023. The game still runs fine on modern hardware and remains available for purchase.

### 5. Divinity: Original Sin 2

The game that paved the way for Baldur's Gate 3 (same developer, Larian Studios). DOS2 is a tactical CRPG with deep elemental interaction systems — fire spreads, water conducts electricity, oil creates fire hazards. Encounters are puzzles as much as fights.

The storytelling and characters don't quite reach BG3's heights, but the combat is arguably more creative. Excellent co-op.

### 6. Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous

The best Pathfinder adaptation, and the purest high-fantasy CRPG since the classic Infinity Engine games. It's complex — some would say overwhelmingly so — but rewards players who invest in understanding its systems.

The Mythic Power system lets you transform your character into a demon, angel, lich, or other supernatural being as the campaign reaches its mythic conclusion. These paths fundamentally change your abilities and the story.

Not for beginners, but a must-play for tactical RPG veterans.

### 7. Pillars of Eternity 2: Deadfire

Obsidian Entertainment (Fallout: New Vegas, The Outer Worlds) made one of the best CRPGs of the last decade with Deadfire. A nautical adventure across a vast archipelago, with a refined version of the Pillars combat system, outstanding companion writing, and a fascinating setting that blends colonial-era aesthetics with high fantasy.

The ship combat is divisive but everything else is excellent.

### 8. Vampire: The Masquerade — Bloodlines 2

**Note:** Bloodlines 2 finally releases in 2025 after years of troubled development. Early reviews are mixed-to-positive. The original Bloodlines (2004) remains a cult classic for its writing and world-building if you want to experience the universe first.

<Callout type="info" title="Historical Recommendation">
The original Vampire: The Masquerade — Bloodlines (2004) is one of the most uniquely written RPGs ever made. It has significant technical issues (install the fan patch), but the dialogue, faction politics, and vampire clan system are unmatched. Find it on Steam for $20.
</Callout>

### 9. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

Still a landmark open-world RPG almost a decade after release. The Witcher 3's quest design — particularly the side quests, which are as substantial as most games' main stories — remains the gold standard. The DLC expansions (Hearts of Stone, Blood and Wine) are better than most full games.

**Get it:** Complete Edition bundles everything for $40-50 regularly on sale.

### 10. Dragon's Dogma 2

Capcom's 2024 follow-up to their cult hit action RPG. Dragon's Dogma 2 is weird, ambitious, and unlike anything else on this list. The Pawn system (AI companions you design and share with other players), the emergent monster encounters, and the sheer physical weight of combat make it a unique experience.

It has significant technical issues at launch (performance problems, limited fast travel by design) that will turn off some players. For the right audience, it's brilliant.

## Quick Decision Guide

**First RPG? Start with:** Baldur's Gate 3 (BG3 beginner guide [here](/blog/baldurs-gate-3-complete-guide-classes))

**Love Dark Souls / challenge?** Elden Ring is mandatory.

**Want a story-focused experience?** Disco Elysium or The Witcher 3.

**Into MMOs?** FFXIV — the free trial is absurdly generous.

**Love tactical grid combat?** Divinity: Original Sin 2, then Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous.

## What's Not on This List

**Starfield** — Bethesda's space RPG has the bones of a great game but lacks the density and writing quality of their best work. Worth playing on Game Pass if you're curious.

**Cyberpunk 2077** — Post-patches, it's a very good action RPG. Not quite in this tier but close. Phantom Liberty DLC elevated it significantly.

**Final Fantasy XVI** — Excellent action, weaker RPG systems. Fans of the franchise will love it; genre newcomers might prefer something else.
