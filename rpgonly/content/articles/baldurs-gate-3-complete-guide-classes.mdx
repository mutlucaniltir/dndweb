Baldur's Gate 3 is the greatest CRPG ever made. That's not hyperbole — it won basically every Game of the Year award in 2023, and for good reason. But starting a new playthrough can be overwhelming, especially when you're staring at 12 classes and dozens of subclasses before you've even left character creation.

This guide breaks down every class in BG3, what they're good at, and which you should pick based on how you want to play.

<Callout type="tip" title="New to D&D rules?">
BG3 is built on D&D 5th Edition rules. If you're new to the system, don't worry — the game explains most mechanics in-game. But knowing the basics will help you make better decisions. Check out our [D&D Starter Kit guide](/blog/dnd-starter-kit-what-you-need) for a quick primer.
</Callout>

## Quick Class Tier List for New Players

Before the deep dive, here's a practical tier list based on **ease of play for beginners**:

| Tier | Classes |
|------|---------|
| Beginner Friendly | Fighter, Ranger, Cleric, Paladin |
| Moderate Complexity | Rogue, Druid, Bard, Warlock |
| Complex / High Reward | Wizard, Sorcerer, Monk, Barbarian |

This isn't about power — all classes can clear the game. It's about how much you'll need to manage.

## The 12 Classes, Explained

### Fighter — The Safe Choice

If you want to hit things hard and not worry too much about systems, pick Fighter. You get heavy armor, great weapon options, multiple attacks, and the Action Surge ability (extra actions on demand) early on.

Fighters are the class that "just works." You'll rarely feel outclassed, and your action economy is excellent throughout the entire game.

**Best subclass for beginners:** Champion (crits more often) or Battle Master (adds tactical maneuvers)

**Avoid if:** You want to feel like a spellcasting mastermind.

### Paladin — Power + Roleplay in One Package

Paladins might be the most complete package in BG3. You get:

- Heavy armor and melee combat (great survivability)
- Divine Smite (add radiant damage on any hit by spending spell slots)
- Aura of Protection (huge passive bonus to saves for you *and* nearby allies)
- Meaningful Oath choices that affect your story

The Oath system is one of BG3's best features. Your Paladin swears an oath that defines their moral code — break it and you become an Oathbreaker with an entirely different set of dark powers. It creates genuine roleplay stakes.

**Best subclass for beginners:** Oath of the Ancients (nature-themed, great defensive aura) or Oath of Devotion (classic paladin)

### Cleric — The Most Versatile Class

Clerics are secretly one of the best classes in the game. Depending on your Domain (subclass), you can be a frontline warrior (War Domain), a heal-bot (Life Domain), a nature caster (Nature Domain), or a terrifying death-powered tank (Spore Domain, Death Domain).

Every party benefits from having Cleric spells available. Even if you're not running a dedicated Cleric, consider multiclassing 1-2 levels in for the armor proficiency and spell access.

**Best subclass:** Life Domain (best healer), War Domain (battle cleric — can wear heavy armor and cast spells), or Light Domain (incredible AoE damage)

### Rogue — Surgical Precision

Rogues deal enormous single-target damage through Sneak Attack — an extra pool of dice you add to a hit whenever you have advantage or an ally adjacent to the enemy. Late game, this can reach 9d6 additional damage per turn.

They're also the skill experts. If you want to pass every Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, and Perception check, Rogue (especially Arcane Trickster) is your pick.

**Best subclass:** Thief (extra Bonus Action — insanely strong), Arcane Trickster (magic + sneakiness)

### Wizard — Full Spellcasting Power

Wizards have the largest spell list in the game and can learn new spells by finding scrolls throughout the world — a massive advantage that other casters don't have. If you love optimizing spell combinations and want maximum magical flexibility, Wizard is your class.

The complexity: you'll spend a lot of time managing spell slots, concentration spells, and positioning. But the payoff — Control spells that lock enemies in place while your party destroys them — is immensely satisfying.

**Best subclass:** Evocation (raw damage), Divination (manipulate die rolls — broken good), Abjuration (defensive specialist)

### Sorcerer — Metamagic Flexibility

If Wizard is the scholar, Sorcerer is the natural talent. Smaller spell list, but Metamagic allows you to modify spells on the fly: extend them, twin them (target two creatures), make them subtle, quicken them (cast as a bonus action).

Sorcerer's real power is in Metamagic combinations. Twinned Spell on Haste (give two allies extra actions) is one of the most powerful combinations in the game.

**Best subclass:** Wild Magic (chaotic random effects, great for fun runs), Draconic Bloodline (survivability + damage bonus)

### Warlock — The Eldritch Powerhouse

Warlocks have a unique resource system: very few spell slots (1-2 at a time), but they recharge on a short rest. This means you cast at full power more reliably than other casters but need to rest more.

Eldritch Blast + Agonizing Blast is one of the most consistent damage outputs in the game — a ranged spell attack that scales to do excellent single-target damage every single turn.

<Callout type="lore" title="The Patron">
Unlike other spellcasters, Warlocks get their power from a deal with a powerful entity — a Fiend, an Archfey, or the Great Old One. In BG3, your patron matters to the story and certain NPCs will react to your Warlock powers. Choose your patron based on which story resonates with you.
</Callout>

### Barbarian — The Rage Machine

Barbarians are simpler than they look. Rage (increased damage resistance, damage bonus, can't concentrate) + hit things with big weapons. The genius is in the Primal Path (subclass) — Berserker adds even more attacks, Wildheart gives animal powers, and Totem Warrior provides utility.

Barbarians are great if you want to be the unstoppable frontliner who shrugs off damage.

### Ranger — The Versatile Hunter

Rangers are BG3's most underrated class. They get Hunter's Mark (bonus damage to a target that follows them), great skill access, and at level 4+ become powerful through the Hunter subclass or Gloom Stalker (which is genuinely top-tier).

Gloom Stalker Rangers with Darkness spell and Darkvision is one of the strongest combat builds in the game.

### Druid — Nature's Complexity

Druids can shape-shift (Wild Shape), cast spells, and do decent melee damage in certain forms. The Moon subclass lets you transform into powerful animals that scale as you level, essentially giving you an extra health pool.

Druids are complex to play well but incredibly satisfying. They're also surprisingly tough.

### Bard — The Face Character

Bards are the best skill characters, have access to any class's spell list via Magical Secrets, and provide powerful support through Bardic Inspiration. If you love talking your way through encounters and want maximum dialogue options, play a Bard.

College of Lore gets extra spell picks earlier, College of Swords is a melee sword dancer fantasy.

### Monk — The Ki User

Monks are the most mechanically unique class. No weapons needed — your fists become your arsenal. Ki points fuel special attacks, extra movement, and powerful reactions. Monks are fragile but extraordinarily mobile.

**Best for:** Players who want a martial arts playstyle. Requires more attention to position and resource management than Fighter.

## Which Class Should You Pick?

Here's the honest advice:

**Play Paladin** if you want a story-rich, powerful, beginner-friendly experience. The Oath system makes every choice feel weighty.

**Play Fighter** if you want to focus on exploration and story without combat management overhead.

**Play Wizard** if you want to feel like a powerful spellcaster and enjoy the optimization puzzle.

**Play Bard** if this is your first RPG and you want maximum story involvement.

## A Note on Multiclassing

BG3 supports multiclassing — taking levels in multiple classes. This adds incredible flexibility but also complexity. For your first playthrough, **don't multiclass**. Learn one class's toolkit fully before you start mixing.

The most popular multiclasses (Paladin/Warlock "Padlock," Fighter/Rogue, Sorcerer/Wizard "Sorwizard") are for second playthrough optimization, not first.

## Final Thoughts

BG3 rewards experimentation. Don't stress too hard about the "optimal" class — every class has viable paths through the game. The best class is the one that makes you excited to keep playing.

What makes BG3 special isn't combat — it's how your character's background, class, and choices shape every dialogue, every encounter, every outcome. Pick the fantasy that excites you and let the story unfold.
